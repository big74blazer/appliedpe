About Applied PE

Applied PE was founded to bring real-world industrial experience, engineering rigor, and audit-defensible documentation to Texas utility tax exemption studies. This is not a spreadsheet exercise — it is an operational and engineering evaluation built to withstand scrutiny.

The firm is led by an Engineer-in-Training with over a 15 years of hands-on experience in the mining, aggregates, and ready-mix concrete industries. Since entering the industry in 2011, work has spanned roles from field supervision through executive leadership, including serving as a Vice President and Project Manager for one of the largest mining companies in the world.

Applied PE has successfully performed and supported predominant use exemptions for sand and gravel operations as well as ready-mix concrete plants, delivering permanent utility tax savings through compliant, well-documented studies aligned with Texas Tax Code requirements.

Academic credentials include a Bachelor of Science in Geological Engineering from the University of Missouri – Science & Technology, along with Mineral Extractives study at the University of Derby providing a strong technical foundation in extraction, material processing, and industrial operations.

Why This Matters in an Audit

Texas predominant use exemptions are frequently reviewed years after they are claimed. In an audit, assumptions, estimates, and undocumented classifications are often the first items challenged.

Applied PE studies are built to withstand that scrutiny. Engineering calculations are supported by on-site verification, visual documentation, and clearly traceable links between meters, equipment, and production processes.

By combining engineering judgment with drone-verified site mapping, we provide a defensible record of how electricity is actually consumed — reducing exposure to tax, penalties, and interest if reviewed by the Texas Comptroller.
